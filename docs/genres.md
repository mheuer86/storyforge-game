# Storyforge Genres

Six worlds. Six ways to lose everything. Pick one.

---

## Space Opera

**Tagline:** A fractured galaxy. One ship. No good options.

**Thesis:** Scale mismatch -- small people, big stakes. The protagonist is almost always underscaled for the stakes they end up facing. They aren't chosen heroes. They're the people who happened to be standing in the right place when something broke, and who couldn't quite walk away.

### Setting

Year 3187. The Compact held 200 star systems together for centuries. It doesn't anymore.

What's left is a galaxy of competing scavengers: Compact Remnants clinging to legitimacy and the beacon corridors they built, pirate fleets carving territory and running protection rackets, corporate blocs that bought out Compact infrastructure during the collapse, and frontier settlements too small to be a faction but too numerous to ignore. Rogue AIs occupy derelict stations and network nodes since containment protocols failed during the fracture. FTL travel exists but is constrained by beacon corridors -- charted routes the Compact built. Whoever controls a beacon controls the traffic through it. Some corridors have gone dark entirely.

You command a scrappy frigate with a small crew, navigating this chaos. The crew is what makes the scale mismatch survivable. A solo freelancer facing sector-scale stakes is a tragedy. A crew facing it together is a story. A campaign that begins with a smuggling run can end at a meeting with an entity older than the Compact, but only if you kept saying yes to things that mattered.

Technology runs on pulse weapons, FTL drives, cybernetic augments, neural interfaces. Credits, not gold. Hacking, not spellcasting. Med-patches, not potions.

### Origins (5)

| Origin | Description | Advantage | Vulnerability |
|--------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Human** | The default face in every station and port. Nobody looks twice. | Blend in, pass unnoticed, avoid profiling | No cultural network to fall back on |
| **Vrynn** | Insectoid. Chitinous plating, compound eyes. Uncommon in core systems. | Perception checks (compound eyes process movement faster) | Disadvantage on initial Persuasion with species that rely on facial expressions |
| **Korath** | Broad, dense-boned, grey-skinned. Built for heavy gravity worlds. | Engineering and Athletics checks | Disadvantage on Deception -- your culture doesn't train for lying |
| **Sylphari** | Tall, luminescent markings. From tidally locked worlds where patience is survival. | Insight checks (reading emotional states) | First impression with frontier or military NPCs starts one tier lower |
| **Zerith** | Lean, scaled, cold-blooded. No homeworld, no unified government. | Initiative and Reflex saves | Authorities and law enforcement start at Wary |

| Class | Concept | Primary | Trait | Cost |

| **Vanguard** | Frontline Soldier / Tank | STR | **Adrenaline Surge** -- bonus attack after a successful hit (1/day) | Straightforward. |

| **Ace** | Pilot / Sharpshooter | DEX | **Dead Eye** -- treat one ranged attack as a critical hit on 19-20 (1/day) | Also has vehicle expertise: advantage on all piloting checks. |

### Asset: Ship

The frigate is the party's home, weapon, and escape route. Ship systems run at levels 1-3:

- **Engines** -- L2: -2 piloting DCs. L3: -4, can always escape.
- **Weapons** -- L2: 1d10. L3: 1d12 + boarding capability.
- **Shields** -- L2: -1 damage per hit. L3: -2 + deflect one per encounter.
- **Sensors** -- L2: detect hidden threats. L3: reveal enemy intent.
- **Crew Quarters** -- L2: cohesion +1 at chapter start. L3: +1 cohesion + companions recover 1d4 HP.

Hull damage runs -15 to -25 per hit. Below 30%: disadvantage on piloting. Chapter-end refits offer 2-3 upgrade options embedded in narrative.

### Hooks

- **Black Box** -- A routine salvage job turns up a black box from a ship that went missing during the Compact fracture. The data is military-grade encrypted, and three factions are already asking where you found it. Keeping it is dangerous. Selling it is profitable. Listening to it might change everything.
- **Dead in Space** -- A refugee transport is dead in space. They can't pay. Your fuel says you can tow them or continue your job, not both. And the job is the one that pays this month's docking fees.
- **Neutral Ground** (Diplomat) -- Two factions on the verge of war meet on your ship. Then the lead negotiator is found dead in their quarters, and both delegations are blaming the crew.
- **Inside Job** (Technomancer) -- The ship's AI woke you at 0300: someone accessed the encrypted partition while you were asleep. The intrusion came from inside the ship. The AI can't identify who.

## Epic Sci-Fi

**Tagline:** Power has a price. Someone always pays.

**Thesis:** Institutional complicity. You are inside a machine that runs on human lives. The question isn't whether the system is wrong -- it's what you do while you're part of it.

### Setting

The Hegemony has endured for a thousand years. It holds together because of Resonants -- humans born with the ability to attune to the Drift, a substrate beneath normal spacetime. Resonants power everything: FTL travel, planetary shields, communication, weapons. They are identified at age seven, taken by the Synod, and deployed as imperial infrastructure. Their power is immense. Their legal status is property.

The Great Houses control territory and compete for Resonant allocation the way old empires competed for oil. The Synod -- part state religion, part regulatory body -- controls attunement testing, training, and deployment. It claims the Drift is divine. It hunts unregistered Resonants. The Throne balances both factions and enforces a stability that looks, from a distance, like peace. The Undrift -- unregistered Resonants -- survive in hiding, sell their abilities on the black market, or form networks that the Synod classifies as heresy.

What the Synod suppresses: sustained attunement degrades Resonants. They call it the Dimming. The Ashen Wards house spent Resonants far from public view, classified as non-functional, filed away where the system doesn't have to look at what it did to them. Every institution serves itself while claiming to serve the whole. You are inside this machine, and the machine needs you to keep turning the gears.

### Origins (6)

| Origin | Description | Advantage | Vulnerability |
|--------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Minor House** | House Vael. Inside the machine, low on the ladder. Your family's Resonant allocation was halved two generations ago. | Persuasion involving trade, negotiation, alliance-building | Any Major House NPC's initial disposition capped at Neutral |
| **Synod-Raised** | Taken to the Synod's scholarium before you can remember. You know the testing protocols and what the Synod keeps for itself. | INT checks on Resonant lore, Drift phenomena, Synod procedure | The Synod tracks its own. Leaving is permitted but noted. Word of betrayal reaches them in days. |
| **Undrift** | Unregistered Resonant. Every institution wants to own, use, or destroy you. | Access to the Undrift network: black-market suppressants, safe houses, unregistered services | Hunted. Any public Drift use risks a Synod detection roll. |
| **Imperial Service** | You serve the Throne directly. Personal loyalty is a vulnerability; political ambition is disqualifying. | Checks involving protocol, jurisdiction, and official authority | Everyone assumes you're reporting everything. Disposition advances capped at Favorable with non-Imperial NPCs. |
| **Ascendant** | Born Untuned, no house, no church. The system wasn't built for you. | Checks where no established protocol exists -- you've never had the luxury of doing things the expected way | No safety net. Your patron periodically calls in favors, and refusing risks losing your only protection. |
| **Spent Resonant** | Survivor of the Ashen Wards. Classified as non-functional. Still here. | Immune to standard Drift detection; institutional knowledge of Synod procedures from the inside of its disposal mechanism | Demonstrating Drift capacity makes your classification fraud. | **Veil** | Spy / Infiltrator | DEX | **Ghost Protocol** -- leave no trace on one security scan, witness memory, or official record (1/chapter) | The erasure is perfect, but the absence can be noticed. A gap in a record is suspicious. |
| **Physik** | Resonant Physician / Bio-Artificer | WIS | **Grim Prognosis** -- heal 1d8+WIS with a side effect: pain, dependency, or temporary sense loss (1/chapter) | Side effects worse on patients with Drift exposure history. |

### Asset: Retinue

The retinue is your growing personal power base, made of people with loyalty, morale, and limits. Five dimensions at L1-L3:

- **Sworn** -- military capacity and personal guard
- **Intelligence** -- information network and advance warning
- **Household** -- administrative support and logistics
- **Drift Capacity** -- Resonant services (upgrading this should trigger moral reflection: you are deepening your claim on a human being's service)
- **Reputation** -- political weight and faction standing

Each upgrade tier should feel like a narrative milestone. Retinue members are named NPCs with opinions and loyalties.

### Hooks

- **The Allocation** -- Your house has been allocated a new Resonant. She's fourteen. She arrives tomorrow. Her family hasn't stopped sending messages. The Synod says to ignore them.
- **Quiet Disposal** -- A Resonant in your service has stopped responding to attunement. The Synod wants them returned. Your commander wants the problem to disappear. The Resonant is sitting in your quarters, lucid, asking you not to send them to the Ashen Wards.
- **Doctrinal Anomaly** (Seeker) -- Your investigation leads to a Resonant who attuned without Synod training. Impossible, according to doctrine. Your report will either rewrite Synod theology or end her life.
- **The Ward** (Physik) -- Reassigned to an Ashen Ward. On your first day, you discover a third of the "spent" Resonants still have measurable attunement. They're not burned out. They're being hidden.

## Grimdark

**Tagline:** Kingdoms rot from the inside. Someone has to survive it.

**Thesis:** Moral entropy. Every choice costs something. Victories are never clean. The question isn't whether you'll compromise, it's how much of yourself you'll have left when you're done.

### Setting

The Shattered Provinces have held peace for a generation under the Pact of Ashes, but the treaty is fraying like wet rope. A famine called the Wasting spreads from the southern provinces, crops failing and livestock dying without explanation that anyone in power wants to accept. Border lords raise private armies. The Church of the Pale Flame controls hospitals, orphanages, and the Inquisition, and it burns reformers and calls it salvation. Something stirs beneath the old ruins that predate every kingdom alive today.

You command a small mercenary company navigating a world where old allegiances mean less every week. This is mud, blood, iron, and rot. Weapons are swords, axes, and crossbows. Magic exists but it is feared, costly, and rarely clean. There are no heroes here, only survivors and the bodies they left behind. Humor is dark, sharp, and necessary -- the kind that keeps people sane when the alternative is screaming.

The common people suffer regardless of who rules. The question is never "who's right?" It's "who's paying the price for someone else's ambition today?"

### Origins (5)

| Origin | Description | Advantage | Vulnerability |
|--------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| **House Veldran** | Merchant nobility. Wealth is the only bloodline that matters. | Commerce, bribes, reading financial motives | Common folk start at Wary. First transaction with any new NPC at disadvantage until non-financial relationship established. |
| **House Sylvara** | Ancient woodland bloodline. Long-lived, patient to a fault. | Perception and Insight checks | Disposition advances require two positive interactions instead of one. Frontier and common-folk NPCs start at Wary. |
| **House Stonemark** | Mountain clan. Dense-boned, blunt-spoken, legendary craftsmen. | CON saves and Engineering/Crafting checks | Disadvantage on Deception. Your culture considers lying a form of cowardice. |
| **The Oathless** | Broken oath. Expelled, fled, or disgraced from a landed house. | Survival outside institutional structures | Disadvantage on initial social checks with any landed house. One contact from former house at Hostile. |
| **House Ashfang** | Fallen bloodline. Scaled, fast, remnants of the Char Dominion that burned three centuries ago. | Intimidation and Initiative | Most NPCs start at Wary. Persuasion relying on good faith (not leverage) at disadvantage. The Church starts at Hostile. |

### Classes (6)

| Class | Concept | Primary | Trait | Cost |
|-------|---------|---------|-------|------|
| **Cutthroat** | Assassin / Shadow Operative | DEX | **Marked** -- designate a target; first hit deals +1d6 bonus damage (1/day) | The choice of who to mark is the moral weight. |
| **Ironclad** | Hardened Mercenary / Heavy Weapons | STR | **Last Standing** -- drop to 1 HP instead of 0 (1/day) | No strings. The Ironclad's reliability is the point. |
| **Hexblade** | Forbidden Magic / Curseworker | INT | **Corruption Tap** -- auto-succeed on one forbidden magic check or inflict a minor curse (1/day) | Each use darkens your reputation. Cumulative, never resets. Priests recoil, commoners whisper. |
| **Schemer** | Political Operative / Spy | CHA | **Leverage** -- reveal a secret about a target NPC that changes the conversation (1/day) | Requires prior interaction or intel. The target remembers, and the relationship is permanently changed. |
| **Plague Doctor** | Battlefield Medic / Apothecary | WIS | **Bitter Medicine** -- heal an ally for 1d8+WIS (1/day) | Treatment has a side effect chosen by the GM: nausea, hallucinations, or dependency. Nothing is free. |
| **Inquisitor** | Sanctioned Interrogator / Institutional Authority | INT | **The Question** -- after 1+ rounds of dialogue, force a WIS save; failure reveals one truth (1/day) | On success, they know you tried. Failed interrogations have social consequences. |

### Asset: Company

The mercenary company has five dimensions tracked at L1-L3:

- **Strength** -- fighters, equipment, combat capability
- **Morale** -- will to fight, unit cohesion under pressure (degrades without pay or after defeats)
- **Reputation** -- who knows you, whether they hire or avoid you
- **Intelligence** -- scouts, informants, advance warning
- **Provisions** -- supplies, medical stores, operational endurance (consumed by operations)

The company degrades between chapters without maintenance. Each dimension has a named NPC attached (the sergeant, the quartermaster, the scout). Upgrading a dimension means investing in the person who runs it.

### Hooks

- **The Mercy** -- Three days ago, the company freed prisoners from a lord's dungeon. Good deed, clear conscience. This morning, two of them killed. The lord's steward arrives: "He wasn't keeping them in cages because he's cruel. He was keeping them in cages because he knew what they'd do."
- **Stolen Name** -- A courier brings payment for a job the company didn't do. Someone is using your name, and whoever hired them wants a reckoning.
- **Three More Days** (Ironclad) -- A lord's keep is under siege. You're not reinforcements. You're a mercenary company passing through. But the commander is offering everything he has left to anyone willing to hold the wall.
- **The Magistrate** (Schemer) -- You've been appointed magistrate of a town that executed the last three magistrates. Your first case: a merchant accusing a priest of theft. The priest accusing the merchant of heresy. Both are lying, and the town council is watching to see which lie you choose.

## Fantasy

**Tagline:** The world is older than it remembers. What was lost has a price. Pay it anyway.

**Thesis:** The world is forgetting itself. The knowledge that built civilization is disappearing. Ancient ruins dot every kingdom, libraries no one can read, machines no one can operate. Magic is not ascending; it is echo. Every spell draws on a diminishing reservoir that no one knows how to replenish.

### Setting

The Five Kingdoms still stand. The Accord of Thorns still holds on paper. But the world is ending in forgetting, not in fire.

Ancient ruins are everywhere, and nobody remembers who built them. The Collegium studies what remains but understands less each decade. The churches preach about gods whose names they may have wrong. The wilderness reclaims what civilization can no longer maintain. Border lords dispute treaties written in a legal language no living scholar fully commands. A plague spreads from the eastern marshes, and something stirs beneath ruins that predate every kingdom alive today.

The Dragonkin remember fragments through oral tradition and inherited dream-memory, but even their knowledge is incomplete, fading with each generation. The event called the Sundering severed the world from its own past. Every discovery is a fragment, every spell is borrowed time, and every scholar knows less than the one who came before them. The grandeur is real -- ancient halls, vast forests, the weight of prophecy. The grit grounds it: mud on boots, hunger on the road, and the growing suspicion that the answers you're looking for died with the people who had them.

### Origins (5)

| Origin | Description | Advantage | Vulnerability |
|--------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Human** | The common face of every kingdom. Systems were built by humans, for humans. | Blend in, navigate bureaucracy | No racial network. Other species look after their own; humans are on their own. |
| **Elf** | Slender, long-lived, keen-eyed. Patience isn't passive, it's earned by outliving hasty decisions. | Perception and History checks | Frontier and common-folk NPCs start at Wary. |
| **Dwarf** | Stocky, dense-boned, mountain-born. A dwarf's word is a contract. | Engineering, Appraisal, and CON saves | Disadvantage on Deception. Lying goes against everything your culture taught you. |
| **Halfling** | Small, nimble, deceptively tough. People see the size and stop looking. | Stealth and going unnoticed in crowds | Disadvantage on Intimidation. Nobody fears someone they could pick up. |
| **Dragonkin** | Tall, scaled, remnants of the fallen Wyrm Kingdoms. Carry fragments of pre-Sundering knowledge through inherited memory and oral tradition. | Intimidation, Initiative, and checks involving Ancient ruins or pre-Sundering lore | Most NPCs start at Wary. Climbing to Trusted takes twice as long. |

### Classes (6)

| Class | Concept | Primary | Trait | Cost |
|-------|---------|---------|-------|------|
| **Shadowblade** | Shadow Walker / Threshold Crosser | DEX | **The Price of Shadows** -- vanish through somewhere and bring something back (1/day) | After each use, the GM places a detail in the next scene from wherever you stepped through. Sometimes useful. Sometimes wrong. |
| **Warden** | Sworn Protector / Last Guardian | STR | **Oath Burden** -- bonus attack from invoking a sworn duty (1/day) | Must keep one promise made during the chapter or lose the trait next chapter. Strength bound by obligation. |
| **Arcanist** | Spell Archaeologist / Lost Knowledge Seeker | INT | **Arcane Surge** -- auto-succeed on an Arcana check or force a re-save (1/day) | On nat 1 spell checks, wild magic surges with a random effect (helpful, harmful, or strange). |
| **Herald** | Lorekeeper / Story Wielder | CHA | **Bardic Echo** -- invoke a story, song, or legend that shifts the mood of a scene (1/day) | Requires speaking; useless when silenced. Stories invoked become known to whoever heard them. Skilled NPCs can use that knowledge against you. |
| **Keeper** | Divine Vessel / Fading Thread | WIS | **Divine Favor** -- healing strength tied to deity alignment (1/day) | Acting in alignment: full power + bonus. Acting against: half. The GM tracks favor silently. |
| **Ranger** | Wilderness Interpreter / Living Memory | DEX | **The Ranger's Mark** -- treat one ranged attack as a critical hit (1/day) | Requires studying the target for a full round. NPCs witnessing this feel it. Disposition gains slow for one chapter. |

### Asset: Fellowship

Unlike a ship or retinue, the fellowship is made of people with their own arcs, secrets, and breaking points. Each companion has:

- A reason they joined (personal stake in the chapter's hook)
- A secret that becomes relevant later
- A vulnerability that triggers under specific player decisions
- An arc that resolves alongside the main plot

The fellowship doesn't degrade automatically between chapters (unlike Grimdark), but it fractures when you ignore individual companions' needs. Companions aren't followers -- they're allies with their own reasons for being here. A companion who disagrees is more valuable than one who agrees.

### Hooks

- **The Second Library** -- A scholar at the Collegium dies under suspicious circumstances. Her last notes describe "the second library" and she writes about it as if you've already been there together. You've never met her.
- **The Road** -- An ancient road has appeared in the forest overnight. Stone-paved, perfectly preserved, leading somewhere that wasn't there yesterday. Three woodsmen have walked it. None have come back.
- **Something Answered** (Arcanist) -- Your spell misfired last night. Not a wild surge -- something answered. A voice in the residual energy, speaking words you didn't cast. Your focus crystal is still warm this morning.
- **The Moving Wood** (Ranger) -- The Thornwood is moving. Not growing -- moving. The tree line has advanced half a mile in a week, swallowing farmland. The locals say the forest is angry. The tracks you found at the new edge say something in the forest is hunting.

## Cyberpunk

**Tagline:** The city owns everything. You're about to steal some of it back.

**Thesis:** Humanity vs capability. Every upgrade makes you better at the job and worse at being a person. The city manufactures this trade-off on purpose, because people who feel less are easier to use.

### Setting

The city never sleeps and it doesn't care if you do. Megacorporations own the law, the media, and most of the people worth knowing. You run with a small crew from a safehouse somewhere in the city's grinding middle layers -- not poor enough to be invisible, not rich enough to be safe.

Cities are vertical, stratified by wealth. Cyberware is ubiquitous. The net is a parallel world where consciousnesses operate alongside daemons and security architectures that kill intruders. Street-level life runs on fixers, gangs, mercenary crews, and the black market. Law enforcement is privatized. Privacy is a commodity. The corps don't rule through tyranny; they rule through dependency. The infrastructure you need to survive is the infrastructure they own.

Every run is a transaction: take the job, get the eddies, keep the crew alive long enough to do it again. But the jobs have a way of pulling you into systems bigger than you bargained for, and the chrome you bolt on to survive those systems has a way of making you forget why surviving mattered in the first place.

### Origins (5)

| Origin | Description | Advantage | Vulnerability |
|--------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Street Kid** | Born in the gutter wards. Knows every shortcut, every unwritten rule. | Streetwise checks | Disadvantage on initial social checks with corpo or institutional NPCs. Active liability: gang obligations surface once per chapter. |
| **Corpo Dropout** | Former corporate employee. Polished accent, expensive habits, one bad decision. | Social checks in corporate environments | Gang and street NPCs start at Wary. Active liability: your former corp has a file on you and wants you controllable. |
| **Nomad** | Arrived from the badlands with a car, a gun, and clan instincts. | Survival and vehicle-related checks | City fixers start at Wary. Active liability: clan requests come with emotional weight -- these are family, not clients. |
| **Undercity Born** | Grew up in the maintenance warrens and black-market tunnels beneath the city. | Navigate, hide, or find things in urban infrastructure | Disadvantage in high-society or corporate settings. Active liability: undercity debts that don't convert to eddies. |
| **Syndicate Blood** | Raised inside organized crime. Knows how authority actually works. | Intimidation and reading power dynamics | Acting against syndicate interests gets reported. Betrayal drops every syndicate NPC by two disposition tiers. |

| Class | Concept | Primary | Trait | Cost |
|-------|---------|---------|-------|------|
| **Ghost** | Infiltrator / Social Engineer | DEX | **Zero Trace** -- leave no trace on one security scan, camera network, or access log (1/day) | The absence itself can be noticed. After 3 uses per chapter, Insight checks against human emotion get disadvantage. |
| **Razorback** | Chrome Street Samurai / Enforcer | STR | **Adrenaline Overclocked** -- bonus melee attack after a successful hit (1/day) | Chrome stress accumulates. At threshold (3 uses per chapter without rest), involuntary aggression -- WIS save to resist violent impulses in social scenes. |

| **Medtech** | Combat Medic / Body Modder | WIS | **Field Triage** -- stabilize a downed ally or gain advantage on Medicine checks as a bonus action (1/day) | After 3 uses per chapter without a personal connection scene, bedside manner degrades. NPC patients start at Wary. |
| **Solo** | Contract Killer / Survivor | CON | **Dead Man Walking** -- ignore all damage from one attack (1/day) | You felt it; you just don't stop. |

### Asset: Tech Rig

Personal tech rig uses modular upgrades at levels 1-3:

- **Neurofence** -- L2: auto-deflect first hack per scene. L3: counter-hack.
- **Spectra** -- L2: advantage evading surveillance. L3: full cloak, once per chapter.
- **Redline** -- L2: boost two checks per chapter. L3: no burnout risk.
- **Panoptik** -- L2: detect threats through walls. L3: predict enemy actions.
- **Skinweave** -- L2: corporate-grade ID forgery. L3: full biometric clone.

Integrity runs like hull condition. Below 30%: disadvantage on tech checks.

### Hooks

- **Blackout** -- The city goes dark. Not a power failure -- a targeted blackout in a six-block radius. In the silence, a voice on every local channel says a name. It's yours.
- **Dead Crew** -- A rival crew is dead. Their equipment, their job board, and their contacts are up for grabs -- but so is whoever killed them.
- **Someone Home** (Netrunner) -- You found something in the net that found you back. Not ICE, not a daemon -- a consciousness. It's living in decommissioned infrastructure and won't let you log out until you do something for it. Your deck is burning through battery and your body is getting cold in the chair.
- **Bad Chrome** (Medtech) -- A patient you saved comes back -- different. The cyberware you installed is behaving autonomously. Two more former patients report the same thing. Someone in your supply chain has been slipping modified implants into your inventory.

## Noir

**Tagline:** Everyone lies. The truth is what's left when the lies stop working.

**Thesis:** Information as currency. The mystery isn't in the data -- it's in what someone said, who they said it to, and what they left out. In this city, knowing the truth is as dangerous as the lie it replaced.

### Setting

A city that runs on money, secrets, and the careful distribution of both. The police are overworked or bought. The wealthy are untouchable until they aren't. The streets have their own justice. Everyone has a past, and most of those pasts are the kind you pay to keep quiet.

This is an investigation-driven world. Combat exists but it is desperate and ugly, not heroic. A fist fight in an alley has consequences that last longer than the bruises. INT and WIS are the primary currencies of survival. CHA checks carry lethal weight because the wrong word to the wrong person can end you faster than a bullet. You work alone, mostly. Rain-slicked streets, frosted glass office doors, dive bars where nobody looks up, hotels where the clerk remembers nothing. The kind of city where the truth is always uglier than the lie it replaced.

The cases start simple: a missing spouse, an insurance claim, a favor for a friend. They never stay simple. Every thread you pull connects to something bigger, something someone powerful is paying to keep buried. And every question you ask is noticed by someone who'd rather you didn't.

### Origins (5)

| Origin | Description | Advantage | Vulnerability |
|--------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Ex-Cop** | Former law enforcement. Left for a reason someone can pull. | Predict or navigate law enforcement behavior | Criminals remember your badge. |
| **Street** | Grew up where the papers only come when someone dies. | Streetwise checks | Disadvantage on initial social checks with institutional authority. |
| **Old Money** | From the families that built this city. You know the quiet arrangements. | Social checks in elite settings; leverage institutional access | Disadvantage on initial social checks with working-class and street NPCs. |
| **Veteran** | Went to war and came back different. Violence doesn't shock you. | CON saves against fear and intimidation | Disadvantage on Insight checks reading civilian emotional cues. NPCs notice your lack of reaction to violence. |
| **Immigrant** | Came from somewhere else. You see this city clearly. | Insight checks reading people outside your community | Community promises carry extra weight. Breaking one drops disposition by two tiers. |

### Classes (6)

### Asset: None (Heat System)

Noir has no ship, company, or tech rig. Instead, it tracks **Attention** -- how much powerful people are paying attention to you.

- **None** -- invisible.
- **Low** (noticed) -- NPCs mention your name to each other.
- **Medium** (watched) -- you're being tailed.
- **High** (targeted) -- someone has decided you're a problem.
- **Critical** (hunted) -- they've decided on a solution.

Attention rises from asking wrong questions publicly, intimidating connected people, getting your name in a police report, or getting too close to the truth.

### Hooks

- **Five Names** -- A woman you've never met left your name in her will. She died yesterday. The inheritance is a locked box and a list of five names -- four of them are still alive.
- **2 AM** -- A friend calls at 2am, panicking. By the time you get to their apartment, they're gone. The door is unlocked, the lights are on, and there's blood on the kitchen floor -- but not enough to be fatal.
- **Fifteen Minutes** (Lawyer) -- A man walks into your office fifteen minutes before his arraignment. He says the person who killed his wife is the prosecutor assigned to his case. His hearing starts in fifteen minutes and he wants you to walk in there with him.
- **Playing Along** (Grifter) -- You're halfway through the best con of your career when you realize the target knows exactly what you're doing. They're playing along. That's worse.
