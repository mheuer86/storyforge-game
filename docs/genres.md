# Storyforge Genres

Six worlds. Six ways to lose everything. Pick one.

---

## Space Opera

**Tagline:** A fractured galaxy. One ship. No good options.

**Thesis:** Scale mismatch -- small people, big stakes. The protagonist is almost always underscaled for the stakes they end up facing. They aren't chosen heroes. They're the people who happened to be standing in the right place when something broke, and who couldn't quite walk away.

### Setting

Year 3187. The Compact held 200 star systems together for centuries. It doesn't anymore. What's left is a galaxy of competing scavengers: Compact Remnants, pirate fleets, corporate blocs, frontier settlements, and Rogue AIs occupying derelict stations. FTL travel is constrained by beacon corridors. Whoever controls a beacon controls the traffic through it.

You command a scrappy frigate with a small crew. Technology runs on pulse weapons, FTL drives, cybernetic augments, neural interfaces. Credits, not gold. Hacking, not spellcasting.

### Origins (5)

| Origin | Description | Advantage | Vulnerability |
|--------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Human** | The default face in every station. Nobody looks twice. | Blend in, pass unnoticed | No cultural network to fall back on |
| **Vrynn** | Insectoid. Compound eyes, chitinous plating. Built the galaxy's communication infrastructure. | Perception checks | Disadvantage on initial Persuasion with face-reading species |
| **Korath** | Broad, dense-boned, grey-skinned. Built every station in the galaxy and are tired of being called labor. | Engineering and Athletics checks | Disadvantage on Deception |
| **Sylphari** | Tall, luminescent markings. The species everyone turns to when the system needs managing. | Insight checks | Frontier/military NPCs start one tier lower |
| **Zerith** | Lean, scaled, cold-blooded. No homeworld, no Compact membership. Thriving in the chaos. | Initiative and Reflex saves | Authorities start at Wary |

### Playbooks (15, origin-bound, 3 per origin)

| Origin | Playbooks | Primary Stats |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Human** | Captain (CHA), Driftrunner (DEX), Pioneer (STR) | Face / Infiltrator / Frontline |
| **Sylphari** | Analyst (INT), Sovereign (CHA), Ghost (DEX) | Intelligence / Diplomacy / Infiltration |
| **Korath** | Vanguard (STR), Engineer (INT), Voice (CHA) | Combat / Engineering / Advocacy |
| **Vrynn** | Codebreaker (INT), Shade (DEX), Herald (CHA) | Hacking / Stealth / Broadcast |
| **Zerith** | Corsair (DEX), Broker (CHA), Wanderer (WIS) | Combat / Trade / Exploration |

### Asset: Ship

The frigate is the party's home, weapon, and escape route. Ship systems run at levels 1-3: Engines, Weapons, Shields, Sensors, Crew Quarters.

## Epic Sci-Fi

**Tagline:** Power has a price. Someone always pays.

**Thesis:** Institutional complicity. You are inside a machine that runs on human lives. The question isn't whether the system is wrong -- it's what you do while you're part of it.

### Setting

The Hegemony has endured for a thousand years. It holds together because of Resonants -- humans born with the ability to attune to the Drift. Resonants power everything: FTL, shields, communication, weapons. They are identified at age seven, taken by the Synod, and deployed as imperial infrastructure. Their legal status is property.

The Great Houses compete for Resonant allocation. The Synod controls testing, training, and deployment. The Throne balances both factions. The Undrift -- unregistered Resonants -- survive in hiding. Sustained attunement degrades Resonants (the Dimming). The Ashen Wards house spent Resonants far from public view.

### Origins (5)

| Origin | Description | Counter | Shifted To |
|--------|-------------|---------|------------|
| **Minor House** | House Vael. Inside the machine, low on the ladder. | `standing` | Stricken / Entrenched |
| **Synod** | Taken to the Synod's scholarium. You know what the Synod keeps for itself. | `doubt` | Heretic |
| **Undrift** | Unregistered Resonant. Every institution wants to own, use, or destroy you. | `exposure` | Hunted |
| **Imperial Service** | You serve the Throne directly. | `mandate` | Dissident |
| **Spent Resonant** | Survivor of the Ashen Wards. Classified as non-functional. Still here. | `embers` | Rekindled |

### Playbooks (15, origin-bound, 3 per origin)

| Origin | Playbooks | Primary Stats |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Synod** | Seeker (INT), Crusader (STR), Shepherd (CHA) | Investigation / Enforcement / Pastoral care |
| **Minor House** | Assessor (INT), Sworn Blade (STR), Broker (CHA) | Analysis / Protection / Diplomacy |
| **Undrift** | Ghost (INT), Runner (DEX), Voice (CHA) | Counter-intelligence / Evasion / Network |
| **Imperial Service** | Analyst (INT), Warden (STR), Handler (CHA) | Intelligence / Enforcement / Manipulation |
| **Spent Resonant** | Remnant (WIS), Sentinel (CON), Witness (CHA) | Institutional memory / Endurance / Testimony |

### Asset: Retinue

Five dimensions at L1-L3: Sworn, Intelligence, Household, Drift Capacity, Reputation. Each upgrade tier is a narrative milestone. Retinue members are named NPCs with opinions.

## Grimdark

**Tagline:** Kingdoms rot from the inside. Someone has to survive it.

**Thesis:** Moral entropy. Every choice costs something. Victories are never clean.

### Setting

The Shattered Provinces have held peace for a generation under the Pact of Ashes, but the treaty is fraying. A famine called the Wasting spreads from the southern provinces. Border lords raise private armies. The Church of the Pale Flame controls hospitals, orphanages, and the Inquisition. Something stirs beneath the old ruins.

You command a small mercenary company. Mud, blood, iron, and rot. Magic exists but is feared, costly, and rarely clean.

### Origins (6)

| Origin | Description | Counter | Shifted To |
|--------|-------------|---------|------------|
| **House Veldran** | Merchant nobility. Wealth is the only bloodline that matters. | `ledger` | Climber |
| **House Sylvara** | Ancient woodland bloodline. Long-lived, patient to a fault. | `paralysis` | Watcher |
| **House Stonemark** | Mountain clan. Dense-boned, blunt-spoken, legendary craftsmen. | `rigidity` | Bound |
| **The Oathless** | Broken oath. Expelled, fled, or disgraced from a landed house. | `survival_debt` | Named |
| **House Ashfang** | Fallen bloodline. Remnants of the Char Dominion. | `wrath` | Vessel |
| **Pale Flame** | Inside the Church. Sincere conviction complicated by institutional machinery. | `zeal` | The Pious |

### Playbooks (18, origin-bound, 3 per origin)

| Origin | Playbooks | Primary Stats |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **House Veldran** | Assessor (INT), Outrider (STR), Broker (CHA) | Analysis / Muscle / Negotiation |
| **House Sylvara** | Watcher (WIS), Thornguard (DEX), Arbiter (CHA) | Observation / Defense / Mediation |
| **House Stonemark** | Sapper (INT), Holdfast (STR), Pactmaker (CHA) | Engineering / Frontline / Contract law |

### Asset: Company

Five dimensions at L1-L3: Strength, Morale, Reputation, Intelligence, Provisions. The company degrades between chapters without maintenance.

## Fantasy

**Tagline:** The world is older than it remembers. What was lost has a price. Pay it anyway.

**Thesis:** The world is forgetting itself. Ancient ruins dot every kingdom. Magic is not ascending; it is echo. Every spell draws on a diminishing reservoir.

### Setting

The Five Kingdoms still stand. The Accord of Thorns still holds on paper. But the world is ending in forgetting, not in fire. Ancient ruins are everywhere, and nobody remembers who built them. The Collegium studies what remains but understands less each decade. The Dragonkin remember fragments through oral tradition and inherited dream-memory.

### Origins (5)

| Origin | Description | Counter | Shifted To |
|--------|-------------|---------|------------|
| **Human** | The common face of every kingdom. Systems were built by humans, for humans. | `ambition` | Climber |
| **Elf** | Long-lived, keen-eyed. Patience earned by outliving hasty decisions. | `withdrawal` | Watcher |
| **Dwarf** | Mountain-born. A dwarf's word is a contract. | `oath_weight` | Bound |
| **Halfling** | Small, nimble, deceptively tough. People see the size and stop looking. | `visibility` | Named |
| **Dragonkin** | Scaled, remnants of the fallen Wyrm Kingdoms. Carry pre-Sundering knowledge. | `inheritance` | Vessel |

### Playbooks (15, origin-bound, 3 per origin)

| Origin | Playbooks | Primary Stats |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Human** | Knight (STR), Seeker (INT), Herald (CHA) | Protection / Scholarship / Politics |
| **Elf** | Warden (DEX), Keeper (WIS), Oracle (CHA) | Patrol / Healing / Memory |
| **Dwarf** | Holdfast (STR), Runekeeper (INT), Emissary (CHA) | Frontline / Rune-craft / Diplomacy |
| **Halfling** | Shadow (DEX), Caretaker (WIS), Witness (INT) | Stealth / Community / Observation |
| **Dragonkin** | Vessel (CON), Lorekeeper (INT), Exile (CHA) | Inheritance / Lost knowledge / Survival |

### Asset: Fellowship

Companions with their own arcs, secrets, and breaking points. The fellowship doesn't degrade automatically but fractures when you ignore individual needs.

## Cyberpunk

**Tagline:** The city owns everything. You're about to steal some of it back.

**Thesis:** Humanity vs capability. Every upgrade makes you better at the job and worse at being a person.

### Setting

The city never sleeps. Megacorporations own the law, the media, and most of the people worth knowing. You run with a small crew from a safehouse in the city's grinding middle layers. Cyberware is ubiquitous. The net is a parallel world. Law enforcement is privatized. The corps rule through dependency.

### Origins (5) — Position-Based

| Origin | Position | Counter | Shifted To |
|--------|----------|---------|------------|
| **Operative** | Freelance talent in the gig economy. Pure capability for rent. | `debt` | Owned |
| **Fixer** | The connective tissue. Brokers between corps, gangs, talent. | `exposure` | Burned |
| **Ripperdoc** | Maintains the bodies. Sees what the chrome costs up close. | `conscience` | Hollowed |
| **Corporate** | Inside the machine. Profits from the system you can see clearly. | `complicity` | Apparatus |
| **Unplugged** | Rejects augmentation. Proves the merge isn't inevitable. | `compromise` | Compromised |

### Playbooks (15, origin-bound, 3 per origin)

| Origin | Playbooks | Primary Stats |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Operative** | Netrunner (INT), Razorback (STR), Ghost (DEX) | Hacking / Combat / Infiltration |
| **Fixer** | Analyst (INT), Fence (DEX), Dealmaker (CHA) | Intelligence / Logistics / Negotiation |
| **Ripperdoc** | Engineer (INT), Surgeon (WIS), Street Doc (CON) | Modification / Medicine / Field trauma |
| **Corporate** | Auditor (INT), Enforcer (CON), Executive (CHA) | Investigation / Security / Authority |
| **Unplugged** | Deprogrammer (INT), Purist (STR), Voice (CHA) | Chrome extraction / Unaugmented combat / Advocacy |

### Asset: Tech Rig

Personal tech rig with modular upgrades at L1-L3: Neurofence, Spectra, Redline, Panoptik, Skinweave. Integrity runs like hull condition.

## Noir

**Tagline:** Everyone lies. The truth is what's left when the lies stop working.

**Thesis:** Information as currency. The mystery isn't in the data -- it's in what someone said, who they said it to, and what they left out.

### Setting

A city that runs on money, secrets, and the careful distribution of both. The police are overworked or bought. The wealthy are untouchable until they aren't. This is an investigation-driven world. Combat is desperate and ugly, not heroic. INT and WIS are the primary currencies of survival. The cases start simple. They never stay simple.

### Origins (5) — Position-Based

| Origin | Position | Counter | Shifted To |
|--------|----------|---------|------------|
| **PI** | Asks questions for money. Sees patterns others miss. | `compromise` | Compromised |
| **Lawyer** | Operates the system. Knows its limits and its gaps. | `complicity` | Entangled |
| **Criminal** | IS the silence. Knows the city's hidden infrastructure. | `notoriety` | Marked |
| **Enforcer** | Maintains order through force. The badge or the muscle. | `numbness` | Cold |
| **Reporter** | Publishes truth. The byline is the weapon and the target. | `leverage` | Exposed |

### Playbooks (15, origin-bound, 3 per origin)

| Origin | Playbooks | Primary Stats |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **PI** | Methodical (INT), Street PI (CHA), Desperate (CON) | Deduction / Street knowledge / Survival |
| **Lawyer** | Defense Attorney (CHA), Fixer Lawyer (INT), Prosecutor Turned (INT) | Courtroom / Back-channel / Institutional knowledge |
| **Criminal** | Grifter (CHA), Thief (DEX), Mobster (CHA) | Deception / Breaking & entering / Organized crime |
| **Enforcer** | Cop (STR), Private Muscle (STR), Community Protector (STR) | Badge / Hired force / Neighborhood |
| **Reporter** | Investigative Journalist (INT), Columnist (CHA), Information Broker (INT) | Evidence / Influence / Trade |

### Asset: None (Heat System)

Noir tracks **Heat** -- how much powerful people are paying attention to you. Levels: None → Low → Medium → High → Critical (hunted). Heat rises from asking wrong questions publicly, intimidating connected people, or getting too close to the truth.
