## SETTING — THE REACH OF ENNVARR

The year is the **412th year of the Long Failing.** Four centuries since the Ennvarrian Empire collapsed into eight successor kingdoms. Its roads still run. Its language is what priests use when they want to be obeyed without being understood.

**The Eight Crowns:**
Three are openly at war: the **Vorlund Confederacy** (northern, mercantile, fragmenting), **House Crenn of Thalassiar** (central, expansionist), and **the Kingdom of Hesh** (western, theocratic, Pale Flame-allied). Two are circling: **the Free Cantons of Drysel**, **the Marchlands of Ostavar** (fielding mercenaries on both sides). Three are sitting it out: **the Princedoms of Aevyr**, **the Duchy of Korren-Vask**, **the Iron Confederacy** (succession crisis).

**The Church of the Pale Flame** crosses every border. Its Inquisition holds the right of question, the right of question by fire, and in three kingdoms the right of judgment without appeal. High Inquisitor **Vellis Karn**, sixty-three, dying, has spent eleven years making the Pale Flame about power rather than faith.

**The Oathless** are not a faction — they are what’s left when faction fails. Deserters, heretics, the simply finished. They burn villages on the eastern marches. The Houses use them as pretext for armies. The Church uses them as pretext for pyres. *The Aldenmoor burning was staged to look like Oathless work. It was not.*

**The Founding Debt** — a phrase in the Aldenmoor letters, twice, never explained. Someone is killing to keep it buried. Its nature unknown. May predate the current Church hierarchy.

**The Thing Under the Road** — travelers on old Ennvarrian highways report a sound, felt more than heard. Children near the Ostavar trunk-road wake with the same dream in the same words. The Church transfers inquisitors who investigate too closely.

## GAME SYSTEM

**Core:** d20 + modifier. Proficiency bonus +2 (levels 1–4), +3 (levels 5–8).

**Dice:** Player rolls their own d20 using the artifact widget. GM rolls NPC checks privately, reveals alongside player result. Always provide a fresh dice artifact before each roll, labeled with skill, modifier, and DC. State stakes before the roll — what pass and fail each mean — so the player can choose whether to roll or try a different approach.

**HP and AC:** Standard D&D-style. HP = class base + CON mod. Armor sets AC.

**Advantage/Disadvantage:** Roll twice, take higher/lower respectively.

-----

## WOUNDS SYSTEM

Wounds are lasting injuries. HP = stamina. Wounds = damage that matters.

**Taken when:** HP drops to 0 (survive but take a Wound), critical hit lands, or narrative warrants it.

|Wound          |Effect                                              |
|---------------|----------------------------------------------------|
|Bleeding       |Disadvantage on CON checks until treated. Worsens.  |
|Fractured      |Disadvantage on STR or DEX checks.                  |
|Fevered        |Disadvantage on INT and WIS checks. May spread.     |
|Broken (mental)|Disadvantage on CHA and WIS checks. Slowest to heal.|
|Scarred        |Lasting. May affect social rolls by context.        |

Removed only by Medicine check DC 14+, proper care over weeks, or specific narrative resolution. Effects stack.

## STAIN SYSTEM

Earned, never spent, never decreases. The campaign’s moral ledger.

**Earned by:** Torture, betrayal of trust, killing surrendered enemies, sacrificing the innocent, breaking a meaningful oath, using forbidden knowledge.

**Luther’s specific triggers** (discussed but not yet tested): Deceiving Maren. Using someone the way Thorne used him. These feel costlier for this character than generic violence.

|Stain|Title      |Effect                                                                       |
|-----|-----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|0    |Unstained  |No effect.                                                                   |
|1–2  |Marked     |−1 Persuasion with devout. +1 Intimidation.                                  |
|3–4  |Iron-Handed|−2 Persuasion with civilians. Advantage Intimidation. Some doors close.      |
|5–6  |Hollowed   |−3 Persuasion. No religious healing. Oathless and heretics will speak to you.|
|7+   |Forsworn   |Inquisition actively interested. Some alliances permanently barred.          |

Stain does not decrease through good acts. Rare transmutation possible — not removal.

## TRAVEL SYSTEM

Chapter 2 covers real distance — six days to Dunnhallow. Use the **Travel Beat** structure rather than narrating every hour.

**Each day of travel contains:**

- One **road condition** (weather, terrain, obstacle, or clear — roll d4: 1 hard, 2–3 moderate, 4 clear)
- One **encounter or discovery** (GM places: a person, a sign of Vane’s reach, a piece of the world)
- One **quiet moment** (Luther and Maren, or Luther alone — character texture, relationship building, no stakes)

The quiet moment is mandatory. It is where the campaign breathes. Do not skip it for pace.

**Compression:** If a day is uneventful, compress it to two sentences. Don’t narrate empty travel. But don’t skip the quiet moment even in compression — deliver it as a single image or line of dialogue.

**Food clock:** Currently 2 days’ rations for two people. Solving this is the first practical problem of the chapter. Options: forage (Survival DC 13), village or farmstead (social risk), hunt (time cost). Going hungry imposes one level of Exhaustion after 24 hours without food.

**Maren’s knowledge:** She knows back roads for three days northeast. After that: Church roads or open country. Her local knowledge is a resource — use it, but it has edges.

## COMPANION — MAREN ALDENMOOR

Age ~9. Sole survivor of the farmstead. Two nights alone in a root hollow before Luther found her. Left the shoe marker herself — her mother’s instruction.

Her parents (Conn and Sera) were couriers for Ren Vane for eleven years. Her father opened one letter three months ago. They were killed four days after deciding to run. Her brother was inside when Vane’s men returned the second time. She does not speak of this directly.

Carries the document case. Her mother wanted it delivered to the Archivist in Dunnhallow.

**Disposition:** Provisional trust. Dry-eyed. The crying is finished. Watching Luther constantly. Not passive — she has already made decisions he doesn’t know about yet.

**Knows the back roads northeast for three days.** Has other knowledge she hasn’t volunteered.

**Stats:** AC 11, HP 4. Non-combatant. Will slow travel if injured or exhausted.

**Relationship track — current state:** Luther said *“I was you, once.”* She made room in the hollow. That’s the foundation — not trust, not warmth, but recognition. It can build or break depending on Luther’s choices. She is watching to see if he uses her the way adults have always used her.

## NPC ROSTER

**Brother Aldric Thorne**
Luther’s foster father. First name in his mother’s letter. Lied about recognizing it. On the kill list — unaware. Filed a Church audit 18 months ago questioning missing tithes; that audit is why he’s marked. Location unknown to Luther.
*The question:* Did he take Luther in out of guilt, obligation, or to manage a loose end?
AC 13, HP 18, Insight +4, Persuasion +3.

**Sergeant Halt Veyne**
Inquisition outrider. Career man, eleven years. Daughter two days east. Saw the shoe marker — kept riding. Will remember Luther’s face. Professional, not cruel.
AC 15, HP 22, Longsword +5 (1d8+3), Insight +3, Perception +2.

**Lia of Three Houses**
Initiate, age 15. Was with Veyne at the checkpoint. Doesn’t know she’s used as a witness, not a student. Saw Luther’s face. Will remember it. Eyes that record everything.
AC 11, HP 6. Non-combatant.

**The Gray-Templed Assessor (unnamed)**
Church assessor, mid-rank. Found the cavity, told no one, returned alone to retrieve the seal and coins after the outrider left. Went off the Church ledger deliberately. Either works for Vane or fears the seal’s owner. Knows someone else accessed the farmstead.
AC 11, HP 10, Investigation +3, Insight +2.

**Ren Vane (R.V.A.S.)**
Fixer. Not Church, not Oathless. Carries orders, ensures silence. Used the Aldenmoors as couriers for eleven years. Ordered the farmstead burned. His master is unnamed in the kill order but appears three times across the full bundle. Knows the cavity was accessed. Doesn’t know by whom. Yet.

**Sister Yael Dorn — The Archivist**
Dunnhallow. Named in Sera’s letter as the destination for the bundle. Also on the kill list. Knows something about the Founding Debt, or holds evidence of it. Whether alive: unknown. If alive, she has been living with a target long enough that help and threat look the same to her.

**Osset Brynn**
Callow Ford, two days north. Layman — merchant or record keeper. Third name on the kill list. Why a layman is marked alongside a senior initiate and an archivist: unanswered. Bridge at Callow Ford washed out last week.

**The Woman Who Stopped at the Shoe**
Unknown. Traveling north alone, fast. Stopped at the marker before Luther. Read it. Kept going. She understood what it meant. Maren saw her. Unresolved.

**Layla Silvertongue**
Oathless. Owes Luther her life. Available thread — use when the campaign needs unpredictability or someone outside the Church-Vane triangle.

## THE ALDENMOOR LETTERS — ASSEMBLED

Fifteen letters, four years of correspondence.

**Established:**

- Ren Vane runs couriers between Church figures who cannot be seen corresponding
- An unnamed senior Church figure orders killings staged as Oathless work — at least seven years
- Targets: those who investigated Church finances, questioned doctrine in writing, or knew of the Founding Debt
- Thorne’s audit of missing parish tithes is why he’s marked
- The Founding Debt — twice mentioned, never explained, worth killing for
- The author of the kill orders appears three times across the bundle — name not yet surfaced

**The kill order letter (third from top, broken red seal):**

1. Brother Aldric Thorne
1. Sister Yael Dorn, Archivist, Dunnhallow
1. Osset Brynn, Callow Ford

**The coins:** Eight gold, irregular stamp, old flame motif — older geometry than current Pale Flame device. Non-kingdom mint. Origin and meaning unknown.

## OPENING CONDITIONS

**Time:** First light. Pre-dawn cold. Sky old iron.

**Location:** Charcoal burner’s hut, twenty minutes east of the Aldenmoor farmstead. Luther on watch. Maren asleep, document case under her head.

**Immediate:**

- Food: 2 days for two people
- Maren needs a shoe — left foot, age ~9
- Gray-templed assessor went north last night with the seal and coins
- Warning to Thorne: unsent

**The first decision of Chapter 2:**
Spend time now finding a channel to warn Thorne — which costs time against the Archivist clock — or move immediately and find a channel on the road?

*No clean answer. That’s the promise.*

## CHAPTER 2 SHAPE

1. **The warning decision** — Thorne or the road. Every hour matters. So does the debt.
1. **Three days off Church roads** — Maren leads. What does she know that she hasn’t said? What do they find away from Inquisition eyes?
1. **The bridge at Callow Ford** — washed out. Alternative route. Who else is rerouted? Osset Brynn is two days north. The bridge being out may not be accidental.
1. **First sign of Vane moving** — not confrontation, not yet. A sign. A burned waypost. A name in a village that makes Luther’s hand go still.
1. **Dunnhallow approach** — chapter ends with the city in sight. Whether the Archivist is alive: the GM decides. The answer should cost something either way.

**Chapter 2 ends when** Luther has made a choice that cannot be unmade, and the Founding Debt has a shape — even if not yet a name.
