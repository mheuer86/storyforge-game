## What to build

Capture first-observation identity and profile facts when an NPC appears, then protect them from same-day reintroduction drift.

The 2026-05-10 Forty Thousand playthrough introduced Maret with one gender in opening prose, then shifted pronouns later. It also described "he is older than the profile" on the second same-day meeting, when that observation should either have been made the first time or framed as newly learned profile data. Existing invariants protect pronoun and age after they are anchored, but they do not guarantee the first visible introduction anchors the fields.

## Definitions

- **First observation** - the first turn in which an NPC is visibly present or directly introduced to the player.
- **Identity anchor** - stable NPC identity fields such as pronoun, approximate age/age band, role, affiliation, and voice/register.
- **Profile fact** - a durable comparison baseline known to the PC or supplied by state, such as dossier age, official role, expected appearance, or "profile says younger."
- **Reintroduction drift** - a later turn presenting a same-day known NPC as if newly observed or newly profiled without a state-visible reason.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] When an NPC is first introduced in prose, Archivist or a deterministic extractor captures pronoun when the prose provides one.
- [ ] Age or age-band is captured when the prose or profile comparison provides it.
- [ ] Profile comparison facts are stored explicitly when they matter, instead of being left as one-off prose.
- [ ] Invariants protect identity anchors after first write, including pronoun and age/profile fields.
- [ ] Narrator receives enough recent identity context to avoid "new character intro" language for an NPC seen earlier the same day or scene.
- [ ] If a later observation contradicts the first observation, the system either rejects it or requires a state-visible explanation such as disguise, bad profile, mistaken identity, or newly accessed dossier.
- [ ] A fixture covers Maret/Mareth first introduction with pronoun and age/profile anchors captured.
- [ ] A fixture covers a second meeting the same day and rejects or repairs "older than the profile" as a fresh intro unless profile data was newly obtained.
- [ ] Existing `npc-identity-anchors` and pronoun-drift fixtures still pass.
