## What to build

Promote objective-resolved close/reframe behavior from the SF2B path into core SF2 and remove the hard minimum-turn dependency as a close permission gate.

The 2026-05-10 Forty Thousand playthrough showed the failure mode: the player accepted the job and acted toward departure, satisfying the chapter's own `closeWhenAny` contract, but core SF2 still treated turn 18 as the earliest normal close. The runtime then created more lien, clearance, and review pressure to keep the chapter alive. That made the chapter feel like an administrative trap instead of a story moving forward.

The fix is not "close every chapter on turn 1." The fix is to make chapter objectives strong enough to carry play, and then let state-derived objective resolution close, reframe, or promote a successor pressure when the objective actually lands.

## Definitions

- **Foreground objective** - the chapter-scale question currently carrying play. Usually the spine thread or a structured foreground objective line.
- **Objective answered** - the foreground objective is resolved, failed, deferred, displaced, or satisfied by a structured `closeWhenAny` gate.
- **Very early answer** - the foreground objective lands before the chapter has produced enough playable consequence to feel like a chapter. This should require explicit successor/reframe pressure, not a numeric hard stall.
- **Close/reframe gate** - runtime-derived signal that tells Narrator and chapter pressure to close, reframe, or promote a successor instead of extending the old question.
- **Objective quality gate** - Author validation that rejects objectives whose likely resolution is a trivial first-turn acknowledgement, unless the objective explicitly carries a successor pressure or reframe path.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Core SF2 computes foreground objective state without requiring `experimentMode: "sf2b-hook"`.
- [ ] `MIN_CLOSE_TURN` no longer blocks close/reframe when the foreground objective has landed and enough chapter consequence exists.
- [ ] A very early objective answer produces one of: explicit successor pressure, explicit reframe candidate, or a validated reason to close immediately.
- [ ] Author validation or normalization catches weak objectives that can be satisfied by a single first-turn acknowledgement without a successor/reframe path.
- [ ] `pacing_contract.close_when_any` is made mechanically usable enough for runtime to detect satisfied close gates, or is backed by structured objective gates that map to threads/procedures/state.
- [ ] Runtime close readiness distinguishes "target turn range" from "close permission."
- [ ] Narrator per-turn context receives a strong close/reframe directive when the gate fires.
- [ ] The hard target range remains useful as pacing guidance for unresolved objectives, but cannot force a resolved objective to keep producing new locks/reviews/fees.
- [ ] A Forty Thousand fixture covers the PC accepting the job around turn 2-5 and verifies the chapter closes, reframes, or promotes departure/action pressure instead of repeating lien clearance until turn 18.
- [ ] A negative fixture covers a false early close: a player says "okay" on turn 1, but the actual foreground objective has not yet produced a decision, consequence, or successor pressure.
- [ ] Existing SF2B objective-gate fixtures still pass or are consolidated into the core gate.

## Agent Brief

**Category:** bug
**Summary:** Let core SF2 close or reframe when the foreground objective has actually landed.

**Current behavior:** Core SF2 treats target/minimum turn count as a close-permission gate for normal closure. The objective gate that can override this currently lives behind SF2B mode, so a resolved chapter objective can keep generating new blockers until the old turn floor is reached.

**Desired behavior:** Core SF2 should compute foreground objective state and distinguish target pacing from close permission. When the objective is resolved, failed, deferred, or displaced with enough playable consequence, the runtime should close, reframe, or promote a successor pressure instead of stretching the old question.

**Key interfaces:** chapter close readiness projection, foreground objective/tension-score selection, pacing contract close gates, successor/reframe directives, Author objective validation, Narrator per-turn close guidance, replay fixture assertions.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Core SF2 can use objective-resolved close/reframe behavior without SF2B experiment mode.
- [ ] Resolved objectives are not blocked solely by the old minimum turn count when there is enough consequence.
- [ ] Very early answers require a successor, reframe, or explicit validated immediate close reason.
- [ ] Fixtures cover Forty Thousand acceptance/departure pressure, false turn-1 closure, and SF2B parity or consolidation.
- [ ] Existing chapter close and pressure replay fixtures still pass.

**Out of scope:** Closing every chapter as soon as the player acknowledges the hook, removing pacing targets, or replacing chapter pressure with fixed mission beats.
