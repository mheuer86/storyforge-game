HARROW SPUR — DOCKING ARM 4, CLAMP KIOSK
The strip-light above kiosk seven flickers twice, catches, holds. It's been doing that since you docked — three days ago, when the hold first appeared in your queue notification and you told yourself it was a processing error.

It is not a processing error.

The display reads 40,000 CR — ADMINISTRATIVE HOLD — DOCKING ARM 4 — CLAMP ACTIVE. Behind the scratched observation panel to your left, the Driftrunner sits exactly where you left her: sealed, grounded, going nowhere. The clamp housing on her forward strut has a green status light. Green means locked.

Behind the kiosk window, Port Assessor Coll — Harrow Spur's procedural face for everything involving clamp authority on arm four — pulls up a second screen without looking at you. She's mid-fifties, uniform pressed out of habit rather than pride, and she has the particular stillness of someone who has explained fee codes to desperate people every day for twenty years and stopped feeling the weight of it around year three.

"Hold reference four-dash-seven-nine-one." She reads it aloud even though you didn't ask. "Administrative. Forty thousand, two hundred credits. Fee cycle refreshes—" she checks the second screen— "in approximately four hours."

Your comm has one unread message. Flagged urgent. Sender listed as The Sable Margin.

You haven't opened it yet.
