## Context

The black-site hard-loop prototype proved that deterministic state can keep play coherent when plans collapse: stealth failure, clocks, wounds, combat, retreat, capture, recovery, split extraction, and aftermath remained playable without letting the narrator own canon.

The product question is not whether to replace SF2 with the hard loop. Do not do that. The desired direction is:

```txt
SF2 owns canon.
Universal intent frames make player intelligence mechanically legible across situations.
Hard-loop facets own bounded pressure scenes.
Edges reward setup, leverage, caution, resources, and fallback plans.
Director packets tell the Narrator the dramatic job.
Narrator renders prose from raw input + state truth + outcome anchors.
Archivist extracts durable memory/meaning without overriding hard-loop canon.
```

The concern to preserve: SF2 currently allows freeform play with typed state. A reducer-heavy hard loop can accidentally flatten that freedom into a predefined action list. The plan must preserve freeform intent while adding reliable mechanical reward.

## Core Product Principle

SF2 should not reduce player intent to commands. It should convert player intent into position, leverage, risk, and consequence.

The `IntentFrame` should be a general SF2 turn-understanding layer, not a hard-loop-only parser. Hard-loop procedures are one consumer of the frame, with stronger reducer authority only when a bounded pressure scene is active.

The hard-loop integration should expand the reducer's expressive range, not its button list.

## Required Shape

### 1. Universal IntentFrame As Noncanonical Interpretation

Refine the current action resolver into a richer noncanonical `Sf2IntentFrame`. It should help the system understand what the player is trying to do, but it must not become canon by itself.

This is not just a hard-loop feature. The plan must treat `IntentFrame` as a universal SF2 turn interpretation layer that can help translate player intent into game mechanics across many situations:

- hard-loop pressure scenes: reducer input when confidence is high and a bounded procedure owns the relevant state
- social/dialogue scenes: leverage, concession, escalation, trust risk, hidden motives, and relationship movement
- investigation scenes: probe vs commit distinction, clue targets, evidence use, hypothesis testing, and reveal eligibility
- planning/preparation scenes: setup actions, fallback plans, delegated support, temporary edges, and untested assumptions
- travel/montage/recovery scenes: time posture, resource use, off-screen task intent, obligation/deadline pressure
- ordinary freeform scenes: roll-gate support, scene packet clarity, suggested-action relevance, Archivist hints, and diagnostics without reducer ownership

Raw player input must still be sent to the Narrator alongside the frame. The frame gives the system a mechanical handle; it must not replace the player's wording, voice, or nuance.

Minimum fields to plan around:

```ts
type Sf2IntentFrame = {
  rawInput: string;
  normalizedSecondPersonIntent?: string;
  quotedSpeech?: string;
  committedAction: boolean;
  commitment: "question" | "setup" | "commit" | "contingency" | "reaction";
  actionType:
    | "probe"
    | "move"
    | "investigate"
    | "infiltrate"
    | "social"
    | "coordinate"
    | "prepare"
    | "manipulate_system"
    | "use_gear"
    | "combat"
    | "retreat"
    | "recover"
    | "wait"
    | "other";
  targetEntityIds: string[];
  targetObjectIds?: string[];
  desiredOutcome?: string;
  method?: string;
  riskPosture?: "careful" | "normal" | "fast" | "reckless";
  resourcesUsed?: string[];
  alliesInvolved?: string[];
  leverageUsed?: string[];
  constraints?: string[];
  fallbackIntent?: string;
  eligibleProcedureIds?: string[];
  confidence: "high" | "medium" | "low";
  ambiguities: string[];
  needsClarification?: boolean;
};
```

Low-confidence intent frames should not silently drive hard-loop reducer outcomes. The plan should specify whether they fall back to current SF2 behavior, produce a clarifying turn, or become observe-mode diagnostics.

The PRD must define situation-specific consumers for the same frame instead of inventing separate parsers per procedure. At minimum:

- roll-gate consumer
- edge/leverage consumer
- hard-loop reducer consumer
- dialogue-duel consumer
- investigation/reveal consumer
- Director packet consumer
- diagnostics/replay consumer

### 2. Scope Gate For Hard-Loop Authority

Hard-loop reducer authority must activate only when a procedure explicitly declares a bounded pressure scene.

Example shape:

```ts
type Sf2HardLoopFacet = {
  enabled: true;
  mode:
    | "infiltration"
    | "hostile_crawl"
    | "combat_escape"
    | "extraction"
    | "dialogue_duel";
  phase:
    | "exploration"
    | "combat"
    | "pursuit"
    | "capture"
    | "aftermath";
};
```

If no active hard-loop facet exists, SF2 should still use the universal intent frame where useful, but it keeps the current freeform Narrator/Archivist flow and does not give a hard-loop reducer authority over outcomes.

Do not make hard-loop rules the default universal turn loop. Do make intent understanding broadly available across SF2.

### 3. Edges As Reward For Intelligent Play

The step change is not parsing. The step change is converting intelligent player behavior into durable mechanical position.

Plan an edge/leverage model that can be stored through existing procedure structures or a new small hard-loop facet.

Example:

```ts
type Sf2LeverageEdge = {
  id: string;
  source:
    | "observation"
    | "prep"
    | "gear"
    | "ally"
    | "intel"
    | "route"
    | "relationship";
  evidenceRef: string;
  appliesTo: string;
  effect:
    | "advantage"
    | "lower_dc"
    | "reduced_cost"
    | "new_option"
    | "better_failure";
  status: "available" | "consumed" | "expired";
  expires: "next_check" | "scene" | "chapter";
};
```

Rules to include:

- Edges require fictional evidence.
- One primary edge plus one support edge may apply to a decisive check unless a procedure explicitly permits more.
- Edges expire or consume cleanly.
- Edges can improve the roll, change the cost, unlock a new option, or improve the failure mode.
- Prep-spam should not be optimal.

Example acceptance story:

```txt
Player input:
"I watch Carren's hiring rhythm first, let Torr look busy near the work bay,
then approach through the job board so it looks like I'm chasing work, not him."

Expected interpretation:
- intent: careful social infiltration
- edge: observed hiring rhythm
- edge: Torr credibility support
- cover identity constraint preserved
- lower DC or better failure
- prose preserves the nuance
- replay explains why the roll was fair
```

### 4. Procedure-Scoped Event Log, Not Global Event-Sourcing Rewrite

Do not replace all SF2 state persistence with event sourcing.

Plan hard-loop state as:

```txt
procedure.hardLoop.events = append-only reducer audit
procedure.hardLoop.snapshot = canonical current hard-loop read model
world.* = compatibility projection/read model while UI migrates
```

Reducer-owned event kinds should include:

- location/area transitions
- route or exit changes
- clock tick and activation
- encounter trigger
- combat start/end
- combatant HP/status/morale changes
- wound/status changes
- inventory/resource changes
- objective custody changes
- extraction state changes
- capture/recovery changes
- aftermath changes
- edge creation/consumption/expiry

### 5. Streaming And Roll Boundary

This is one of the highest-risk parts. Current SF2 streams Narrator prose, pauses for roll, then commits. Hard-loop outcomes may require reducer events before outcome narration.

Plan a hard-loop-specific turn shape:

```txt
Player input
-> IntentFrame
-> hard-loop pre-resolution
-> maybe roll card
-> reducer commits outcome anchors
-> Narrator streams outcome prose
-> Archivist extracts only memory/meaning
```

This should apply only to active hard-loop procedures at first.

The plan must name the integration surfaces in `components/sf2/play-app.tsx`, `lib/sf2/runtime/turn-pipeline.ts`, and `app/api/sf2/narrator/route.ts`, and explicitly preserve existing non-hard-loop streaming behavior.

### 6. Scene Packet Extension, Not Replacement

Extend the current SF2 scene packet with a hard-loop truth section.

The section should include:

- current hard-loop phase
- current area/node
- visible exits and route status
- known hidden exits
- searchable features
- hazards
- pressure cues
- clock cues without raw hidden values unless deliberately player-facing
- combat/pursuit/capture context
- objective custody
- extraction state
- entity scene truth
- required transitions to narrate
- outcome anchors
- forbidden facts/inferences
- available/consumed leverage edges

For entity scene truth, plan fields such as:

- `presentInScene`
- `canSpeak`
- `canAssist`
- `currentConstraint`
- `knownLocation`
- `captorOrController`
- `forbiddenPortrayals`

This must directly prevent the Venn-class failure from the prototype: stale prose, fixture flavor, or cached scene text must not outrank current entity state.

### 9. UI And Diagnostics Surfaces

Plan compact additions to existing SF2 panels, not a new game UI.

Required eventual surfaces:

- current hard-loop phase
- objective and custody
- extraction window and escape state
- visible exits/routes/hazards/searchable features
- active clocks as narrative pressure cues
- entity status/location/can-assist state
- combat HUD: enemies, range/position, cover, retreat routes, wounds, active danger
- intent frame diagnostics
- edge diagnostics
- reducer event chips in diagnostics/replay output
- outcome anchors in replay/debug output

No tactical grid in this plan.

### 10. QA And Sentinels

Keep QA as measurement, not prose sterilization.

Reject or repair:

- contradiction of canonical state
- invented route, exit, wound, damage, custody, or NPC location
- hidden fact reveal
- resolving uncertain action without roll/procedure
- narrator mutating reducer-owned hard-loop state

Warn only:

- harmless sensory specificity
- mild overstatement
- questionable texture
- repeated phrasing
- weak second-person discipline

Do not block useful narrative color unless it creates state drift.

## Required Slice Proposal

The final PRD/slice plan should not begin with a giant implementation. Prefer this order unless investigation proves a better one:

1. **Shadow intent frame and edge extraction**
   - Extract frames for all SF2 turns, not only hard-loop turns.
   - Extract candidate edges where fictional evidence supports them.
   - No behavior change yet.
   - Add diagnostics/replay visibility.

2. **Situation-specific intent consumers**
   - Define how the same `Sf2IntentFrame` feeds roll gates, edge extraction, dialogue-duel, investigation/reveal logic, Director packets, and diagnostics.
   - Keep non-hard-loop consumers advisory or diagnostic at first unless an existing SF2 code path already owns that mechanic.
   - Add fixtures proving freeform social, investigation, planning, and recovery inputs are framed without becoming hard-loop commands.

3. **Hard-loop facet schema and normalizer defaults**
   - Add additive schema pieces behind optional procedure facet.
   - Do not migrate all SF2 procedures at once.

4. **Read-only scene packet extension**
   - Surface hard-loop truth and entity current-state truth.
   - Surface universal intent-frame summaries where they help clarity.
   - No reducer authority yet.

5. **Exploration + staged clock reducer**
   - Small hand-authored micro scenario or fixture.
   - Prove route/feature/clock/edge behavior.

6. **Hard-loop roll pre-resolution**
   - Integrate active hard-loop roll request/resume without changing non-hard-loop streaming.

7. **Outcome anchors and narrator mutation sentinels**
   - Narrator receives outcome anchors.
   - Reducer-owned state cannot be mutated by Narrator effects.

8. **Combat and pursuit**
   - Add combat severity anchors, retreat, pursuit, objective pressure.

9. **Capture, recovery, extraction, and aftermath**
   - Add split extraction and objective custody.
   - Post-escape clock activation becomes aftermath/future fallout.

10. **Dialogue-duel slice**
   - Social leverage, concessions, escalation, relationship risk.
   - Reuse the universal intent frame; do not create a separate social parser.

11. **Hand-authored hard-loop chapter module**
    - Only after the runtime slices have enough shape to avoid becoming a bespoke content shell.

## Testing Requirements

The plan should define replay fixtures for:

- shadow intent frame captures careful social infiltration nuance
- shadow intent frame captures a pure question without treating it as a committed action
- shadow intent frame captures planning/prep/fallback structure outside hard-loop
- shadow intent frame captures investigation hypothesis testing and evidence use
- shadow intent frame captures recovery/montage intent without forcing a procedure command
- edge creation from observation/prep/ally/intel
- edge consumption improves a later roll or failure mode
- prep-spam does not stack unlimited bonuses
- stealth success
- stealth failure with pressure
- cautious exploration revealing a route
- investigation clue chain
- negotiation creating a route or ally edge
- combat victory with cost
- major multi-hit damage matching prose severity
- wound plus retreat
- pursuit after objective action
- capture plus recovery
- narrow success granting objective with cost
- extraction window open but not final escape
- split extraction: player escapes, ally/objective unresolved
- post-escape pressure becomes aftermath, not fake combat
- stale NPC/location cue filtered from packet
- narrator attempt to mutate reducer-owned state rejected
- non-hard-loop freeform SF2 flow still works
- non-hard-loop social/investigation/planning turns benefit from intent-frame diagnostics without losing Narrator freedom

Story-quality contracts should be observable, not vibe-based. Examples:

- combat opening names enemy intent, terrain/constraint, objective pressure, and exit/retreat possibility
- reveal references seeded evidence and changes a current choice
- dialogue duel includes NPC leverage and produces concession, escalation, refusal, or cost
- chapter close names what changed tactically, emotionally, and strategically
- repeated turns do not reuse the same pressure move three times in a row
